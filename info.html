  • Melora, The Wildmother

    • Cedar's True Goddess

      Seek thee out, wisp-bound glade;
      Night Daughters bid thee come.
      To merry dance that has begun;
      Among strange friend so glum.

      Proud Seeded Lion with deep bow;
      Doth greet his mistress kind.
      A burning kiss upon his brow;
      His branches chaos bind.

      Great Owl Beast doth dance alone;
      Heed well his violent reel!
      For in his foot stabs deep a bone;
      Left from his last grim meal.

      The Basilisk about the fray;
      Doth strut and preen and praise.
      But lo, over his admirers weigh;
      The hush of stone lock'd gaze.

      Betixt the trees old Bark-Wight snores;
      Head rest'd upon thick root.
      For ever eager his cup pours;
      The wine of blood soak'd fruit.

      Seek thee not wisp-bound glade;
      For wildfire hath fled.
      The Judging Wood shall your flesh rend.
      Such that new seeds be spread.

    • Gisella's Goal

      Shadow one dreams of night that's full.
      Moonbeams scattered as ropes to pull.
      A tomb atop outlandish spire,
      As birds of prey on branch of ire.
      Buds bloom in her crown of dark.
      Called to the nameless in turn to lark.
      Farewell death's child, be safe always.
      In the darkest night, may you brighten days.

    • For Uraz

      Many went to the Owlord's crown,
      But few returned, came tumbling down.

      "I fear no Owlking said he",
      A Wanderer, fine of fur.
      "We are many, he is one,
      As lucky a battle there ever were."

      But when the owl's song was heard,
      Great as moon and wide as wood.
      The rabbits quite forgot themselves,
      And frozen there like saplings stood.

      The Wanderer, he said the greeting,
      The Rabbits followed through.
      The Farmer bargained for some bread,
      The Child paid the due.

      The Wanderer said "please" and "thank you",
      The Farmer, "How d'you do".
      The Rabbits stole an apple,
      The Child paid the due.

      Many went to the Owlking's crown,
      But fewer returned, came tumbling down.
